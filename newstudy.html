Project Vision

On Jiminy, users can learn about and manage their mental health through meaningful discussions with friends and guidance from insightful resources.

Jiminy provides a platform where users can explore mental wellness independently or collaboratively with trusted friends through a daily check-in in the form of a chat that is guided by an AI moderator.

Team: Sharon Park, Kristie Cordon, Ona Boseroy

Role: User Research, UX/UI Design, Usability Testing

Project Duration: August 2020 - February 2021

Empathize

Kickoff

As social distancing prolonged and more people were losing jobs and living in isolation, we asked ourselves, "Are people ok? How is everyone doing, mentally?" The collective chaos on social media indicated, seemingly not too well.

Growing up in cultures that did not openly discuss mental wellness, we three women of color decided to inquire and more importantly, do something about it.

What are the challenges?

1) Remote

This project was inspired by the effects of social isolation, therefore the team also had to convene and collaborate remotely, from recruiting candidates for research and to testing multiple times for usability and iteration.

2) Ethical

Data collection and sharing has been a problem in existing mental health apps (Talkspace; dangers of collecting sensitive data and misuse of data), and we needed to create a space that users can trust and feel safe in.

3) Monetary

Since this is an app with the intention to help users focus on their mental health, we wanted to avoid ads. From a business standpoint, how would this product be able to make a provit?
